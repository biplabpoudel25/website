About

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the University of Missouri–Columbia, specializing in deep learning and computer vision. My work spans cryo-EM particle picking, histopathology image analysis, and fluorescence-based oil-spill sensing. I focus on building reliable and explainable ML systems that bridge science, health, and environmental applications.

Research

My research interests include few-shot learning, vision foundation models, interpretability, and scientific imaging. I develop efficient deep learning methods that generalize across domains with limited supervision.
